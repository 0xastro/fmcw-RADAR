15 /* The chirp design for the MRR TI design is as follows.
16  *
17  * The Radar operates in two modes - essentially two subfram-
18  * es per frame, with alternate subframes belonging to one of
19  * the two modes.
20  * 1. A low range-resolution (30cm), high max range (80m),
21  * high max velocity(55kmph), poor angle resolution medium
22  * range radar (MRR) mode - called the MRR80 mode.
23  * 2. A high range-resolution (4.5cm), low max range (20m)
24  * low max velocity (18kmph), high angle resolution ultra
25  * short range (USRR) mode - called the USRR20 mode.
26  *
27  * However the max velocity of 80m is not enough for the MRR
28  * use case, and we need to facilitate algorithmic techniques
29  * to improve it. We will create two kinds of chirp in the
30  * MRR80 mode with have a max unambiguous velocity of 55kmph
31  * and the other having a max unambiguous velocity of
32  * (55/1.2) kmph, and then use the 'chinese remainder
33  * theorem' to estimate the true velocity.
34  *
35  * The MRR80 consists of one profile, two kinds of chirps,
36  * with each chirp being repeated 64 times. These two sets of
37  * chirps are processed seperately. The estimate of velocity
38  * in one set, is compared with the estimate in the other set
39  * to generate the true velocity. Only one tx is active in
40  * this mode. i.e. No MIMO is used in MRR80.
41  *
42  * USRR20 consists of one profile (much higher BW MRR80), two
43  * chirps (in a TDM-MIMO configuration), processed as a conventi-
44  * nal two-tx four-rx capture. Some improvements to the max-
45  * velocity is done so that we can still maintain a reasonable
46  * 36kmph max velocity.
47  *
48  * Since the chirp design of MRR80 and USRR20 are different
49  * two different 'processing paths' are used to process them.
50  *
51  * USRR20 is processed by POINT_CLOUD_PROCESSING path and
52  * MRR80 is processed by the MAX_VEL_ENH_PROCESSING path.
53  *
54  */
